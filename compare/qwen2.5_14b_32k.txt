## We Spent Three Weeks Deciding Who Could Decide

We often assume that once we agree on a target architecture, the hard part is over. But navigating who gets to decide what can be just as tricky and
time-consuming.

---

### Xin Yao and her colleagues find themselves in a familiar bind: trying to finalize decisions about their upcoming conference without clarity on
roles or authority.

Xin, an organizer of DDD Europe, joins You (the host) and Alberto Brandolini for a candid discussion that delves into the chaos of decision-making
amid rapidly evolving technology. The conversation reveals how complex systems like AI can both streamline and complicate collaborative design
processes in software architecture.

---

### Deciding on AI Talks

When discussing submissions for AI-related talks, it becomes clear that while they're excited about the influx of innovative ideas, there's no clear
protocol for vetting or accepting these submissions. Xin mentions needing "AI bullshit detectors," but as You points out, this tool might
inadvertently disqualify genuine contributions. The team struggles to strike a balance between embracing new technologies and maintaining quality
standards.

### Workshop Planning

Xin shares that Marco’s workshop on AI-driven software development is nearly sold out due to growing interest in the field. However, she expresses
concern about how these sessions will translate into practical skills for attendees who might not have prior experience with such tools. The
conversation highlights the challenge of bridging gaps between theoretical knowledge and hands-on application.

### Balancing Expertise

Alberto brings up a significant issue: the changing landscape of roles within tech companies as AI takes over repetitive tasks. He notes that
developers are increasingly expected to understand domain expertise, stakeholder needs, and long-term trade-offs—a shift from mere coding to guiding
complex implementations. This transition leaves some feeling obsolete while others struggle to adapt their skills.

### Decision Fatigue

Throughout the conversation, it’s evident that the team is grappling with decision fatigue—trying to decide who can make what decisions without
clear guidelines. Xin worries about the future of mentorship in a rapidly changing tech landscape, and Alberto shares his concern for new graduates
struggling to find jobs despite being brilliant coders.

### Moving Forward

Despite these challenges, there's an underlying optimism about how AI can empower teams to tackle large-scale projects efficiently. The conversation
ends with plans to bring Jabe (a notable expert) on board soon to finalize decisions regarding the conference agenda and speaker line-up.

---

## Analysis
- **Role Ambiguity**: As technologies like AI evolve rapidly, clear definitions of roles become increasingly blurry. Developers are expected to do
more than just coding—understanding domain specifics and guiding stakeholders is now part of their job.
- **Decision-making Complexity**: Deciding who has the authority to make what decisions becomes a complex issue as projects grow in scope and
technology evolves quickly.
- **Mentorship Challenges**: Traditional mentorship roles are disrupted, leading to uncertainty about how to prepare newcomers for tech careers that
may look very different in a few years.

### Final Reflection
The conversation brings to light the nuanced challenges of integrating new technologies into established workflows. While AI promises significant
advancements, it also raises fundamental questions about decision-making and role clarity within teams. The team’s struggle highlights the need for
adaptable frameworks that can evolve alongside technological progress.
